## 1. Course Objective

Upon completion of this course, the graduate will meet all aeronautical knowledge and experience requirements for the Instrument Rating — Airplane as prescribed in 14 CFR Part 141, Appendix C, and will demonstrate the proficiency required to safely conduct IFR operations in actual and simulated instrument meteorological conditions (IMC), meeting FAA Instrument Rating ACS standards.

---

## 2. Regulatory Basis

| Requirement | Reference |
|-------------|-----------|
| Course approval | 14 CFR §141.55 |
| Training content | 14 CFR Part 141, Appendix C |

| Knowledge Test | FAA-CT-8080-3 (IRA) |
| Simulator credit | 14 CFR §141.41 (up to 20 hrs in qualified FTD/AATD) |

---

## 3. Entry Requirements

- Hold a Private Pilot Certificate (ASEL or higher)
- Logged at least 50 hours of PIC cross-country flight time
- Valid FAA Medical Certificate (3rd Class minimum)
- English language proficiency
- Completed PTC enrollment application and records review

## 5. Stage Structure

### Stage 1 — Basic Instrument Flying
**Objective:** Student develops proficiency in attitude instrument flying, basic navigation, and cockpit resource management under IFR.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 1)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G1-1 | IFR regulations — FARs 61, 91, AIM IFR operations | 1.5 |
| G1-2 | Instrument systems — gyroscopes, pitot-static | 1.0 |
| G1-3 | Weather — fronts, IFR weather, icing, thunderstorms | 1.5 |
| G1-4 | IFR charts — en route low, departure procedures | 1.5 |
| G1-5 | IFR flight planning and filing | 1.0 |
| **Stage 1 Ground Total** | | **6.5 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 1)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual Hrs |
|--------|-------|----------|
| F1-1 | Attitude instrument flying — primary/supporting method | 1.5 |
| F1-2 | Straight/level, climbs, descents, turns by instruments | 1.5 |
| F1-3 | Unusual attitude recovery | 1.0 |
| F1-4 | VOR tracking, intercepts, holds | 1.5 |
| F1-5 | GPS navigation, direct-to, flight plan loading | 1.5 |
| F1-6 | Partial panel operations | 1.0 |
| **Stage 1 Flight Total** | **8.0 hrs dual** |

**Stage 1 Completion Standards:**
- Maintain IFR altitude ±200 ft, heading ±20°, airspeed ±15 kts
- Track VOR course within ±10°; execute holding pattern entry/corrections
- Recover from unusual attitudes smoothly within ACS tolerances

---

### Stage 1 Check
**Evaluator:** Assistant Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Oral (30 min) + dual instrument flight (1.5 hr)  
**Areas:** Basic instrument maneuvers, VOR/GPS navigation, holds, partial panel, unusual attitudes  
**Standard:** ACS Instrument Rating tolerances

---

### Stage 2 — Instrument Approaches and IFR Procedures
**Objective:** Student gains proficiency in ILS, VOR, RNAV/GPS, and LOC approaches; IFR departure, en route, and arrival procedures.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 2)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G2-1 | Approach procedures — ILS, VOR, LOC, RNAV (GPS) | 2.0 |
| G2-2 | Standard instrument departures (SIDs), STARs | 1.0 |
| G2-3 | Approach charts — IAP plate reading | 1.0 |
| G2-4 | Missed approach, alternate airport requirements | 1.0 |
| G2-5 | ATC IFR clearances, communication procedures | 1.0 |
| **Stage 2 Ground Total** | | **6.0 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 2)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual Hrs |
|--------|-------|----------|
| F2-1 | ILS approach — full procedure, precision | 1.5 |
| F2-2 | VOR approach — full procedure, circling | 1.5 |
| F2-3 | RNAV (GPS) approach — LPV/LNAV | 1.5 |
| F2-4 | LOC approach + back course | 1.5 |
| F2-5 | Holds — published and non-published | 1.0 |
| F2-6 | Missed approach procedures — all types | 1.0 |
| F2-7 | IFR XC — 250 nm, 3 different approaches | 3.0 |
| F2-8 | IFR operations in low visibility / simulated IMC | 2.0 |
| **Stage 2 Flight Total** | **13.0 hrs dual** |

**Stage 2 Completion Standards:**
- Complete ILS approach to DH ±100 ft, centerline within ±¾ dot
- Complete RNAV approach within ACS tolerances
- Demonstrate proper IFR communications and clearance compliance
- Execute missed approach procedures correctly

---

### Stage 2 Check
**Evaluator:** Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Oral (45 min) + dual IFR flight (2.0 hr)  
**Areas:** Approach procedures (ILS, GPS, VOR), holds, missed approaches, IFR communications  
**Standard:** ACS Instrument Rating tolerances throughout

---

### Stage 3 — IFR Proficiency and Checkride Preparation
**Objective:** Student demonstrates overall IFR proficiency across all ACS task areas and is prepared for the FAA Practical Test.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 3)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G3-1 | ACS review — all instrument task areas | 2.0 |
| G3-2 | IFR weather decision making — case studies | 1.0 |
| G3-3 | IFR risk management, automation management | 1.0 |
| G3-4 | Mock oral examination | 2.0 |
| **Stage 3 Ground Total** | | **6.0 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 3)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual Hrs |
|--------|-------|----------|
| F3-1 | Maneuver consolidation — all approaches | 2.0 |
| F3-2 | Circling approaches, contact approaches | 1.5 |
| F3-3 | Full mock IFR checkride | 2.5 |
| F3-4 | Deficiency corrections | 1.0 |
| **Stage 3 Flight Total** | **7.0 hrs dual** |

---

### Stage 3 Check (End-of-Course Test)
**Evaluator:** Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Written test (50 questions, 70%) + oral (60 min) + flight (2.5 hr)  
**Areas:** All ACS Instrument Rating task areas  
**Standard:** All items satisfactory per ACS

---

## 6. End-of-Course Test Requirements

| Component | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Written Test | 50 questions, minimum 70% |
| Oral Examination | All ACS instrument areas; unsatisfactory = re-test |
| Flight Test | Full ACS instrument rating maneuvers |
| FAA Knowledge Test | Passed (IRA) prior to Practical Test |
| Logbook Hours | 35 hrs instrument, 250 nm IFR XC, 50 hrs XC PIC |
