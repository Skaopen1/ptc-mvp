# PTC Part 141 Syllabus — Multi-Engine Rating (MEL Add-On)
**Pilot Training Center | KPOC — Pocahontas Municipal Airport**  
**Regulatory Basis:** 14 CFR Part 141, Appendix B (Add-On Rating)  
**Aircraft:** Piper Seneca PA-34  
**Document Version:** 1.0 | Effective Date: ___________  
**Chief Instructor Approval:** ___________________________  

---

## 1. Course Objective

Upon completion of this course, the graduate will meet all aeronautical knowledge and experience requirements for the Multi-Engine Land (MEL) rating as prescribed in 14 CFR Part 141, Appendix B, and will demonstrate the knowledge and skill required to safely operate the Piper Seneca PA-34 as a multi-engine airplane, passing the FAA Multi-Engine Practical Test.

---

## 2. Regulatory Basis

| Requirement | Reference |
|-------------|-----------|
| Course approval | 14 CFR §141.55 |
| Training content | 14 CFR Part 141, Appendix B (add-on rating) |
| Practical Test Standards | FAA-S-ACS-7 (Commercial) or FAA-S-ACS-6 (Private) — Multi-Engine task areas |
| Aircraft type | Piper Seneca PA-34 (non-turbine, no type rating required) |
| POH reference | Piper PA-34 Seneca Pilot's Operating Handbook |

---

## 3. Entry Requirements

- Hold at least a Private Pilot Certificate with ASEL rating
- Instrument Rating (ASEL) strongly recommended (required for commercial MEL PIC for hire)
- Valid FAA Medical Certificate (3rd Class minimum)
- High-performance and complex aircraft endorsements (14 CFR §61.31)
- Completed PTC enrollment application

> Note: The Piper Seneca PA-34 qualifies as both complex and high-performance. Students without these endorsements will complete them as part of this course.

## 5. Stage Structure

### Stage 1 — Multi-Engine Systems and Normal Operations
**Objective:** Student develops understanding of multi-engine aerodynamics, PA-34 aircraft systems, and proficiency in normal multi-engine operations.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 1)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G1-1 | Multi-engine aerodynamics — P-factor, torque, VMC, critical engine | 2.0 |
| G1-2 | PA-34 Seneca aircraft systems — engines, fuel, electrical, hydraulics | 2.0 |
| G1-3 | PA-34 POH — normal/emergency procedures, limitations, performance | 1.5 |
| G1-4 | Weight & balance for PA-34 — multi-passenger loading | 1.0 |
| G1-5 | Engine failure analysis — Vmc demo, accelerate-stop, accelerate-go | 1.0 |
| **Stage 1 Ground Total** | | **7.5 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 1)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual Hrs |
|--------|-------|----------|
| F1-1 | PA-34 introduction — preflight, start, taxi, run-up | 1.5 |
| F1-2 | Multi-engine normal T&Os, climbs, descents, landings | 1.5 |
| F1-3 | Multi-engine traffic pattern and landings — precision | 1.5 |
| F1-4 | Multi-engine performance maneuvers | 1.0 |
| **Stage 1 Flight Total** | **5.5 hrs** |

**Stage 1 Completion Standards:**
- Demonstrate PA-34 preflight correctly and completely
- Fly normal multi-engine maneuvers within private/commercial ACS tolerances
- Articulate PA-34 limitations, V-speeds, and emergency procedures accurately

---

### Stage 1 Check
**Evaluator:** Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Oral (30 min) + dual flight (1.0 hr)  
**Areas:** PA-34 systems knowledge, normal operations, multi-engine aerodynamics  
**Standard:** ACS tolerances for applicable certificate level; accurate emergency procedure knowledge

---

### Stage 2 — Engine-Out Operations and Checkride Preparation
**Objective:** Student develops proficiency in single-engine operations, engine-out procedures, and is prepared for the FAA Multi-Engine Practical Test.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 2)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G2-1 | Engine failure — recognition, identification, securing | 1.0 |
| G2-2 | Single-engine approach and landing — decision points | 1.0 |
| G2-3 | PA-34 emergency procedures — fire, systems failures | 1.0 |
| G2-4 | Multi-engine ACS review — all task areas | 1.5 |
| G2-5 | Mock oral examination | 1.5 |
| **Stage 2 Ground Total** | | **6.0 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 2)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual Hrs |
|--------|-------|----------|
| F2-1 | Engine failure in flight — recognition and securing | 1.5 |
| F2-2 | Single-engine approach and landing (simulated OEI) | 1.5 |
| F2-3 | VMC demonstration — recognition and recovery | 1.0 |
| F2-4 | Mock multi-engine checkride — full ACS run-through | 2.0 |
| F2-5 | Deficiency corrections | 1.0 |
| **Stage 2 Flight Total** | **7.0 hrs** |

**Stage 2 Completion Standards:**
- Identify failed engine correctly using "dead foot / dead engine" method
- Complete single-engine approach and landing safely
- Demonstrate VMC recognition and recovery before loss of control
- Complete all ACS multi-engine task areas satisfactorily

---

### Stage 2 Check (End-of-Course Test)
**Evaluator:** Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Written test (40 questions, 70%) + oral (45 min) + flight (2.0 hr)  
**Areas:** All multi-engine ACS task areas including single-engine operations  
**Standard:** All items satisfactory at applicable ACS level

---

## 6. End-of-Course Test Requirements

| Component | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Written Test | 40 questions, minimum 70% |
| Oral Examination | PA-34 systems, multi-engine aerodynamics, emergency procedures |
| Flight Test | Full ACS multi-engine maneuvers including simulated engine failures |
| FAA Knowledge Test | Included in Commercial (CAX) or Private (PAR) KT — no separate ME KT |
| Logbook Verification | Complex + high-performance endorsements completed; 10 hrs MEL instruction |

## 7. PA-34 Seneca — Critical V-Speeds Reference

| V-Speed | Value | Meaning |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Vmc | 80 KIAS | Minimum control speed, air (OEI) |
| Vyse | 105 KIAS | Best single-engine rate of climb (Blue Line) |
| Vxse | 92 KIAS | Best single-engine angle of climb |
| Vsse | 100 KIAS | Safe single-engine speed |
| Vlo | 140 KIAS | Maximum landing gear operating speed |
| Vfe | 157 KIAS | Maximum flap extension speed |
| Vne | 195 KIAS | Never exceed speed |

> All V-speeds from PA-34 POH. Verify against current aircraft POH before flight.
