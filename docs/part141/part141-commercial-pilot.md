## 1. Course Objective

Upon completion of this course, the graduate will meet all aeronautical knowledge and experience requirements for the Commercial Pilot Certificate — Airplane Single-Engine Land (ASEL) as prescribed in 14 CFR Part 141, Appendix D, and will demonstrate the precision and proficiency required to pass the FAA Commercial Pilot Practical Test (ACS).

---

## 2. Regulatory Basis

| Requirement | Reference |
|-------------|-----------|
| Course approval | 14 CFR §141.55 |
| Training content | 14 CFR Part 141, Appendix D |
| Practical Test Standards | FAA-S-ACS-7 (Commercial Pilot ACS) |
| Knowledge Test | FAA-CT-8080-1 (CAX) |
| Instrument Rating | Required per 14 CFR §61.65 (for CPL ASEL) |

---

## 3. Entry Requirements

- Hold a Private Pilot Certificate (ASEL)
- Hold an Instrument Rating (ASEL) — required to act as PIC for hire
- Minimum 190 hours total time at course completion (Part 141)
- Valid FAA Medical Certificate (2nd Class recommended; 3rd Class minimum)
- English language proficiency
- Completed PTC enrollment application and logbook review

## 5. Stage Structure

### Stage 1 — Commercial Maneuver Foundations
**Objective:** Student transitions from private-level proficiency to commercial-level precision, mastering all ACS maneuvers to commercial standards.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 1)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G1-1 | Commercial regulations — FARs 119, 135, 91 | 1.5 |
| G1-2 | Advanced aerodynamics — load factor, V-speeds, high-performance | 1.5 |
| G1-3 | Aircraft systems in-depth — C172N limitations, POH | 1.5 |
| G1-4 | Weather — advanced, PIREPS, SIGMETs, convective | 1.5 |
| G1-5 | Night operations, physiological factors | 1.0 |
| **Stage 1 Ground Total** | | **7.0 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 1)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual | Solo |
|--------|-------|------|------|
| F1-1 | Commercial maneuvers introduction — steep turns 50° | 1.5 | — |
| F1-2 | Chandelles, lazy eights | 1.5 | — |
| F1-3 | Eights on pylons | 1.5 | — |
| F1-4 | Emergency procedures — commercial standards | 1.0 | — |
| F1-5 | Short/soft field — commercial precision | 1.5 | — |
| F1-6 | Solo maneuver practice | — | 2.0 |
| F1-7 | Pilotage XC solo | — | 2.0 |
| **Stage 1 Flight Total** | **7.0** | **4.0** |

**Stage 1 Completion Standards:**
- Steep turns: ±100 ft, ±10°, ±10 kts bank angle entry/exit
- Chandelles, lazy eights: ACS commercial tolerances
- Precision landings: within 200 ft of target, on centerline

---

### Stage 1 Check
**Evaluator:** Assistant Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Oral (30 min) + dual flight (1.5 hr)  
**Areas:** Advanced maneuvers, emergency procedures, commercial precision landings

---

### Stage 2 — Night, Instrument, and Cross-Country Operations
**Objective:** Student develops night, instrument, and long-distance cross-country proficiency to commercial standards.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 2)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G2-1 | IFR procedures review for commercial ops | 1.5 |
| G2-2 | Long-range XC planning — fuel, alternates, weight | 1.5 |
| G2-3 | Night operations — lighting, physiology, night emergencies | 1.5 |
| G2-4 | Commercial decision making, CRM, risk management | 1.5 |
| **Stage 2 Ground Total** | | **6.0 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 2)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual | Solo |
|--------|-------|------|------|
| F2-1 | Night dual local — precision, emergency | 2.0 | — |
| F2-2 | Night dual XC — 100 nm+ | 2.0 | — |
| F2-3 | Night solo — local (post dual sign-off) | — | 1.0 |
| F2-4 | Instrument dual — partial panel, unusual attitudes | 2.0 | — |
| F2-5 | Instrument approaches — ILS, VOR, RNAV | 2.0 | — |
| F2-6 | Long dual XC — 2+ hrs, planning to commercial std | 2.0 | — |
| F2-7 | Solo XC — 300 nm, 3 airports, commercial planning | — | 3.0 |
| **Stage 2 Flight Total** | **10.0** | **4.0** |

**Stage 2 Completion Standards:**
- Night operations: ACS tolerances, safe night emergency procedures
- Instrument: maintain altitude ±100 ft, heading ±10° (commercial standard)
- XC: within 5 nm of planned course, fuel/time management accurate

---

### Stage 2 Check
**Evaluator:** Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Oral (45 min) + dual flight (2.0 hr)  
**Areas:** Night operations, instrument procedures, XC planning and execution

---

### Stage 3 — Commercial Consolidation and Checkride Preparation
**Objective:** Student achieves ACS commercial proficiency in all task areas and is fully prepared for the FAA Practical Test.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 3)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G3-1 | Commercial ACS review — all task areas | 2.0 |
| G3-2 | Human factors, automation management | 1.0 |
| G3-3 | Risk management scenarios — go/no-go | 1.0 |
| G3-4 | Mock oral examination | 2.0 |
| G3-5 | FAR/AIM review — commercial operations specifics | 1.0 |
| **Stage 3 Ground Total** | | **7.0 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 3)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual | Solo |
|--------|-------|------|------|
| F3-1 | All commercial maneuvers — consolidation | 2.0 | — |
| F3-2 | Solo maneuver practice — commercial standards | — | 2.0 |
| F3-3 | Simulated commercial scenario (full flight) | 2.0 | — |
| F3-4 | Mock checkride — all ACS areas | 2.5 | — |
| F3-5 | Deficiency correction and final polish | 1.5 | — |
| **Stage 3 Flight Total** | **8.0** | **2.0** |

---

### Stage 3 Check (End-of-Course Test)
**Evaluator:** Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Written test (60 questions, 70%) + oral (60 min) + flight (2.5 hr)  
**Areas:** All ACS Commercial Pilot task areas  
**Standard:** All items satisfactory at commercial ACS standards

## 6. End-of-Course Test Requirements

| Component | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Written Test | 60 questions, minimum 70% |
| Oral Examination | All ACS commercial task areas |
| Flight Test | Full ACS commercial maneuvers, all items satisfactory |
| FAA Knowledge Test | Passed (CAX) prior to Practical Test |
