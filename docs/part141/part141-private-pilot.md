## 1. Course Objective

Upon completion of this course, the graduate will meet all aeronautical knowledge and experience requirements for the Private Pilot Certificate — Airplane Single-Engine Land (ASEL) as prescribed in 14 CFR Part 141, Appendix B, and will demonstrate the aeronautical skill and knowledge required to pass the FAA Private Pilot Practical Test (ACS).

## 3. Entry Requirements

- Minimum age: 16 (solo), 17 (certificate issuance)
- Valid FAA Medical Certificate (3rd Class or higher)
- Ability to read, speak, write, and understand English
- Completed PTC enrollment application
- No prior training required (zero-time welcome)

## 5. Stage Structure

### Stage 1 — Flight Fundamentals
**Objective:** Student develops basic aircraft control, situational awareness, and confidence in the traffic pattern.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 1)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G1-1 | Aviation history, PTC orientation, regulations overview | 1.0 |
| G1-2 | Aerodynamics — lift, drag, thrust, weight | 1.0 |
| G1-3 | Aircraft systems — C172N powerplant, fuel, electrical | 1.5 |
| G1-4 | Aircraft documents, airworthiness, preflight | 1.0 |
| G1-5 | Airport operations, ATC communications | 1.0 |
| G1-6 | Flight controls, primary/secondary effects | 1.0 |
| G1-7 | Weather fundamentals — METAR, TAF, winds | 1.5 |
| G1-8 | Weight & balance, performance charts | 1.0 |
| **Stage 1 Ground Total** | | **9.0 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 1)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual Hrs | Solo Hrs |
|--------|-------|----------|----------|
| F1-1 | Orientation flight, cockpit familiarization | 1.0 | — |
| F1-2 | Preflight, startup, taxi, run-up | 1.0 | — |
| F1-3 | Straight & level, climbs, descents, turns | 1.0 | — |
| F1-4 | Traffic pattern, normal T&L | 1.5 | — |
| F1-5 | Slow flight, stall recognition/recovery | 1.0 | — |
| F1-6 | Normal and crosswind T&Ls | 1.5 | — |
| F1-7 | Emergency procedures introduction | 1.0 | — |
| F1-8 | Pre-solo review and maneuver consolidation | 1.0 | — |
| F1-9 | First solo (3 T&Ls, supervised) | — | 0.5 |
| **Stage 1 Flight Total** | **9.0** | **0.5** |

**Stage 1 Completion Standards:**
- Maintain altitude ±150 ft, heading ±15°, airspeed ±15 kts
- Demonstrate coordinated flight; recognize and recover from stalls
- Safely complete solo T&Ls unassisted

---

### Stage 1 Check
**Evaluator:** Assistant Chief Instructor or designee (not the student's primary instructor)  
**Format:** Oral review (30 min) + dual flight evaluation (1.0 hr)  
**Areas:** Preflight, normal operations, traffic pattern, stalls, emergency procedures  
**Standard:** All items must meet or exceed ACS Private Pilot tolerances  
**Unsatisfactory items:** Must be re-trained and re-evaluated before Stage 2 begins

---

### Stage 2 — Cross-Country and Instrument Fundamentals
**Objective:** Student develops cross-country planning and execution skills and basic instrument flying proficiency.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 2)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G2-1 | Navigation — sectional charts, pilotage, dead reckoning | 2.0 |

| G2-5 | Flight computer (E6B) — time, speed, distance | 1.0 |
| G2-6 | Human factors, aeromedical, hypoxia | 1.0 |
| G2-7 | Instrument flight principles — attitude instrument flying | 1.5 |
| G2-8 | XC flight planning exercise | 1.0 |
| **Stage 2 Ground Total** | | **11.0 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 2)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual | Solo |
|--------|-------|------|------|
| F2-1 | Basic instrument maneuvers (under foggles) | 1.0 | — |
| F2-2 | VOR/GPS navigation flight | 1.0 | — |
| F2-3 | Dual XC — local area, 2+ legs | 1.5 | — |
| F2-4 | Dual XC — 50 nm+ with diversion exercise | 2.0 | — |
| F2-5 | Instrument maneuvers — unusual attitudes, partial panel | 1.0 | — |
| F2-6 | Solo XC — 50 nm+ (1 supervised, 1 independent) | — | 2.0 |
| F2-7 | Night dual — local, 10 T&Ls | 1.5 | — |

| **Stage 2 Flight Total** | **9.5** | **4.0** |

**Stage 2 Completion Standards:**
- Navigate via pilotage and dead reckoning within 5 nm of planned course
- Maintain instrument altitude ±200 ft, heading ±20°
- Complete solo XC flights independently with sound ADM

---

### Stage 2 Check
**Evaluator:** Chief Instructor or Assistant Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Oral (45 min) + dual evaluation flight (1.5 hr)  
**Areas:** XC planning, weather decision-making, navigation, instrument proficiency, night ops  
**Standard:** ACS Private Pilot tolerances; sound aeronautical decision-making demonstrated

---

### Stage 3 — Maneuver Proficiency and Checkride Preparation
**Objective:** Student achieves ACS proficiency in all required maneuvers and demonstrates readiness for the FAA Practical Test.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 3)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G3-1 | Regulations review — FAR Parts 61, 91 | 1.5 |
| G3-2 | Weather — advanced, go/no-go decision making | 1.0 |
| G3-3 | Aeronautical decision making, risk management | 1.5 |
| G3-4 | ACS review — all task areas | 2.0 |
| G3-5 | Oral exam preparation and mock oral | 2.0 |
| G3-6 | Systems knowledge — C172N in-depth | 1.5 |
| G3-7 | NTSB accident analysis and CRM | 1.0 |
| **Stage 3 Ground Total** | | **10.5 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 3)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual | Solo |
|--------|-------|------|------|
| F3-1 | Steep turns, chandelles, lazy eights | 1.0 | — |
| F3-2 | Ground reference maneuvers (S-turns, turns around a point) | 1.0 | — |
| F3-3 | Stalls — power-on, power-off, accelerated | 1.0 | — |
| F3-4 | Emergency procedures — engine failure, forced landing | 1.0 | — |
| F3-5 | Short/soft field T&Os and landings | 1.0 | — |
| F3-6 | Solo practice — maneuvers consolidation | — | 1.5 |
| F3-7 | Mock practical test (full ACS run-through) | 1.5 | — |
| F3-8 | Deficiency corrections and final prep | 1.0 | — |
| **Stage 3 Flight Total** | **7.5** | **1.5** |

**Stage 3 / End-of-Course Completion Standards:**  
All ACS Private Pilot task areas completed satisfactorily; no unresolved unsatisfactory items.

---

### Stage 3 Check (End-of-Course Test)
**Evaluator:** Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Written test (60 questions, 70% pass) + oral (60 min) + flight (2.0 hr)  
**Areas:** All ACS Private Pilot task areas  
**Standard:** Meets all ACS performance standards  
**Result:** Chief Instructor endorsement for FAA Practical Test

---

## 6. End-of-Course Test Requirements

| Component | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Written Test | 60 questions, minimum 70% |
| Oral Examination | All ACS task areas — unsatisfactory items require re-test |
| Flight Test | Full ACS maneuvers — all items satisfactory |
| FAA Knowledge Test | Passed (PAR) prior to Practical Test |
| Logbook Hours | Verified: 35 hrs total, 3 hrs instrument, 3 hrs night, 5 hrs solo |

## 8. Instructor Endorsements Required

Per 14 CFR §61.87 and §61.93:
1. Pre-solo aeronautical knowledge test
2. Pre-solo flight training
3. Solo flight in Class B, C, D airspace (if applicable)
4. Solo XC flight
5. Repeated solo XC (if applicable)
6. FAA Knowledge Test
7. FAA Practical Test (§61.39)
