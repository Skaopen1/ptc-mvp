## 1. Course Objective

Upon completion of this course, the graduate will meet all aeronautical knowledge and experience requirements for the Instrument Instructor Rating (CFII) as prescribed in 14 CFR Part 141, Appendix C, and will demonstrate the instructional proficiency required to teach instrument flying, IFR procedures, and instrument approaches to student pilots, meeting the FAA CFII Practical Test Standards.

## 3. Entry Requirements

- Hold a Certified Flight Instructor Certificate (ASEL)
- Hold an Instrument Rating (ASEL)
- Valid FAA Medical Certificate (3rd Class minimum)
- Demonstrated instrument flight proficiency
- Completed PTC enrollment application

## 5. Stage Structure

### Stage 1 — Instrument Instructional Techniques
**Objective:** Student develops techniques for teaching instrument flight, including attitude instrument flying, IFR procedures, and effective instruction in simulated/actual IMC.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 1)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G1-1 | Instrument instruction methodology — how to teach IFR | 1.5 |
| G1-2 | Teaching attitude instrument flying — scan techniques | 1.5 |
| G1-3 | Teaching IFR charts — en route, approach, SIDs/STARs | 2.0 |
| G1-4 | Teaching weather for IFR — briefing, go/no-go | 1.5 |
| G1-5 | Teaching cockpit resource management for IFR | 1.5 |
| G1-6 | Teaching partial panel and emergency IFR procedures | 1.0 |
| **Stage 1 Ground Total** | | **9.0 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 1)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual Hrs |
|--------|-------|----------|
| F1-1 | Teaching instrument scan — basic attitude flying | 1.5 |
| F1-2 | Teaching VOR/GPS navigation from right seat | 1.5 |
| F1-3 | Teaching holds — explanation, entry, correction | 1.5 |
| F1-4 | Teaching partial panel and unusual attitude recovery | 1.5 |
| **Stage 1 Flight Total** | **6.0 hrs** |

**Stage 1 Completion Standards:**
- Accurately explain instrument flying methodology and scan techniques
- Demonstrate ability to instruct basic IFR skills from right seat
- Maintain aircraft control and safety while providing instruction

---

### Stage 1 Check
**Evaluator:** Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Oral (30 min) + dual IFR instruction flight (1.5 hr)  
**Areas:** Instrument instruction technique, attitude instrument flying, navigation teaching method  
**Standard:** Accurate instruction, safe flight, ACS instrument tolerances maintained

---

### Stage 2 — Instrument Approaches Instruction and CFII Checkride Preparation
**Objective:** Student masters teaching instrument approaches, missed approaches, and IFR decision-making, and prepares for the FAA CFII Practical Test.

#### Ground Lessons (Stage 2)

| Lesson | Topic | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| G2-1 | Teaching ILS — precision approach, DH, missed | 1.5 |
| G2-2 | Teaching RNAV (GPS) approaches — LPV, LNAV | 1.5 |
| G2-3 | Teaching VOR/LOC approaches — non-precision MDA | 1.0 |
| G2-4 | Teaching circling approaches and contact approaches | 1.0 |
| G2-5 | Mock CFII oral examination | 2.0 |
| **Stage 2 Ground Total** | | **7.0 hrs** |

#### Flight Lessons (Stage 2)

| Lesson | Topic | Dual Hrs |
|--------|-------|----------|
| F2-1 | Teaching ILS approach — full procedure, briefing | 2.0 |
| F2-2 | Teaching RNAV and VOR approaches | 2.0 |
| F2-3 | Teaching missed approach procedures | 1.0 |
| F2-4 | Mock CFII checkride — full PTS run-through | 2.5 |
| F2-5 | Deficiency correction | 1.5 |
| **Stage 2 Flight Total** | **9.0 hrs** |

**Stage 2 Completion Standards:**
- Brief and teach instrument approaches accurately per FAA approach plate
- Correct student errors appropriately; demonstrate alternate teaching techniques
- Complete mock CFII practical test satisfactorily

---

### Stage 2 Check (End-of-Course Test)
**Evaluator:** Chief Instructor  
**Format:** Written test (50 questions, 70%) + oral (60 min) + flight (2.5 hr)  
**Areas:** All CFII PTS areas of operation  
**Standard:** All items satisfactory; no critical errors in instruction or safety

---

## 6. End-of-Course Test Requirements

| Component | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Written Test | 50 questions, minimum 70% |
| Oral Examination | All CFII PTS areas; instrument instructional technique |
| Flight Test | Full CFII PTS — instrument instruction demonstration |
| FAA Knowledge Test | Passed (FII) prior to Practical Test |
| Logbook Verification | CFI + IR confirmed; 15 hrs instrument instruction received |
