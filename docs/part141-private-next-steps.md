# Part 141 Private Pilot - Curriculum Framework & Next Steps

Expanding the Private pilot syllabus with stage progression, lesson matrix, and operational records.

---

## Overview

**Program Duration:** 60-80 flight hours  
**Ground Training:** 20 hours classroom + self-study  
**Flight Training:** 50-60 hours dual + solo  
**Written Exam (WRITTEN):** 70-100 questions, 2.5 hours  
**Checkride Oral + Practical:** ~3 hours  

---

## Stage Progression & Checks

### Stage 1: Foundations (Flights 1-10)
**Objectives:** Familiarization, basic aircraft control, straight-and-level flight

**Lessons:**
- Flight 1-2: Aircraft systems, preflight, takeoff/landing intro
- Flight 3-5: Straight-and-level, gentle turns, altitude/speed control
- Flight 6-8: Turns, climbs, descents, traffic pattern entry
- Flight 9-10: Shallow turns, speed control, landing consistency

**Stage 1 Check:**
- Perform safe takeoff
- Maintain altitude ±100 ft, heading ±10°
- Execute safe approach and landing
- Demonstrate basic instrument scanning

**Checkpoint Sign-Off Required:** Instructor confirms readiness to Stage 2

---

### Stage 2: Maneuvers (Flights 11-25)
**Objectives:** Advanced aircraft control, maneuver proficiency, basic navigation

**Lessons:**
- Flight 11-15: Steep turns, slow flight, stalls (power-on/power-off)
- Flight 16-18: Emergency procedures, engine failure scenarios
- Flight 19-21: Ground reference maneuvers (rectangular course, turns around a point, S-turns)
- Flight 22-25: Cross-country planning, VFR navigation, pilotage

**Stage 2 Check:**
- Demonstrate all required maneuvers to PTS standards
- Plan and execute short cross-country flight (VFR)
- Identify landmarks and maintain navigation accuracy
- Demonstrate emergency procedures

**Checkpoint Sign-Off Required:** Instructor confirms readiness to Stage 3

---

### Stage 3: Cross-Country & Solo (Flights 26-45)
**Objectives:** Cross-country proficiency, solo flight operations, passenger briefing

**Lessons:**
- Flight 26-30: Longer cross-country flights, fuel planning, weather interpretation
- Flight 31: First solo (local)
- Flight 32-37: Solo flights (3 total with different routes)
- Flight 38-40: Cross-country prep, night flying intro (if VFR-N rating applicable)
- Flight 41-45: Review + practical test prep

**Stage 3 Check:**
- Complete solo cross-country requirement (≥150nm, 3+ landings)
- Demonstrate fuel management and weight-and-balance
- Show proficiency in GPS and sectional chart navigation
- Pass written knowledge test (WRITTEN)

**Checkpoint Sign-Off Required:** Endorsement for checkride eligibility

---

### Stage 4: Checkride Preparation (Flights 46-50+)
**Objectives:** Proficiency review, mock oral, final skill polish

**Lessons:**
- Flight 46-48: Full scenario flights (simulating checkride conditions)
- Flight 49-50: Mock oral exam, stress management, final review

**Checkride Readiness:**
- All maneuvers at PTS standards
- Navigation demonstrated (paper chart + GPS)
- Systems knowledge solid (from oral study)
- Instructor endorsement on file

## Ground Training & Knowledge Areas

**20 Hours Classroom Content:**
1. Regulations & Airspace (2 hrs)
2. Weather & Decision-Making (3 hrs)
3. Aircraft Systems (4 hrs)
4. Navigation & Charts (3 hrs)
5. Weight & Balance (1 hr)
6. Performance Planning (2 hrs)
7. Risk Management (2 hrs)
8. Cross-Country Planning Lab (2 hrs)
9. Practical Exam Review (1 hr)

**Self-Study (Home):**
- FAA Pilot's Handbook (PHAK)
- Airplane Flying Handbook (AFH)
- Practical Test Standards (PTS)
- Online modules (King Schools, Gleim, etc.)
- Recommended: 40+ hours self-study before checkride

## Notes

- **Flexibility:** Students progressing faster may compress timeline; slower learners may need 80+ hours
- **Weather:** Ground/classroom hours fill gaps during unsuitable flying weather
- **Currency:** Solo flights require minimum 3 days between flights; dual training flexible
- **Instructor Continuity:** Primary instructor recommended for all flights to ensure consistent feedback
- **Documentation:** All records must be signed by certified instructor and retained by school for audit/accreditation
